Good morning, Detective Starling.

I'm sorry to disturb you at this time. We all heard about your broken leg down at the precinct, and we hope you get well soon. Unfortunately, as I'm sure you've heard, for the first time in twenty years we've had a murder in Greendale Village.

On Saturday morning a restaurant owner from Lovich Street came barging into the police station. He claims that, on his way to open his diner, he noticed that the bakery across the street still had its lights off and its sign flipped to closed. He found this unusual, as its owner, Nico Galanis, is almost always the first to open shop. Upon crossing the street to check up on Mr. Galanis, the witness found door to the shop was open, and the front window broken. While his speech deteriorated into gibberish from this point forward, it was clear he found the place trashed. Display cases shattered, chairs and tables overturned, emptied cash register, the works. He also saw blood near the back room, where, upon further investigation, we found Mr. Galanis's body.

If it's not too much trouble, we could very much use your help. That big-city expertise of yours will be vital towards bringing this killer to justice! If you can't go out yourself, maybe send out some assistants of yours? I'd very much like to work with lovely Ms. Patterson again.

Again, hope you feel better,
Constable Bill Braddox
